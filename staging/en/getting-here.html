Travelling to Bratislava

Bratislava patrí medzi najmladšie hlavné mestá Európy. Napriek tomu má bohatú históriu slobodného kráľovského korunovačného mesta. Vtedy bola ešte Prešporkom nazývaná. Dnes je Bratislava významný dopravný uzol v strednej Európe a možností, ako sa sem dostať, je neúrekom.

By air

If you are travelling to Bratislava, you can fly to the Bratislava airport (airport code: BTS) or the Vienna airport (airport code: VIE). There are shuttle buses running between Bratislava and Vienna airport every hour and they are cheap (the bus ride costs about EUR 7 one way). The selection of flights to Vienna is better than to Bratislava.

There are two bus companies operating airport shuttles between Vienna airport and Bratislava: SlovakLines (red buses) and Blaguss (silver buses). Blaguss is faster and somewhat cheaper but SlovakLines will take you directly to the bus station. The schedule is at website.

By car

  • From Vienna, take highway A4, heading to Schwechat airport, when possible take exit that leads to highway A6, which is heading to Bratislava.
  • From Budapest, take highway M1 heading to Gyor, go this way until it is possible to switch to highway M15 headed to Slovakia and Bratislava.
  • From Prague, take highway D1 to Brno, once in Brno take highway D2 heading all the way to Slovakia and Bratislava.
  • From Warsaw, take highway A1 heading to Katowice, from there take road bound to Čadca, Slovakia. Take this road all the way to Žilina, then switch to highway D1 heading to Bratislava.

By bus

All neighbouring capital cities Vienna, Prague, Budapest, Warsaw and Kiev have a direct bus connection.

Once you arrive at the Bratislava bus station (Mlynské Nivy), you need to walk through the terminal towards its main entrance. Take a left and walk about 70 meters towards a busy street. Do not cross the street. There will be public transportation bus stops immediately on your side (right side) of the road.

By train

All neighboring capital cities Vienna, Prague, Budapest, Warsaw and Kiev have direct train connection.

Once you arrive to the Bratislava train station (Bratislava hlavná stanica), you need to walk through the terminal towards its main entrance. In front of the main entrance, in about 100 meters there will be public transportation bus stops on your right.

Travelling in Bratislava and to the conference

By car

  • Coming from direction BRNO -> BRATISLAVA from highway D-2, E65 to Lamačská road during Sitina tunnel (or via Patrónka) turn right to Mlynská dolina.
  • Coming from direction NITRA, ŽILINA -> BRATISLAVA from highway D-1, E75 continue in direction Wien, Györ, Brno, turn to Lafranconi bridge - direction Brno to Mlynská dolina.
  • Coming from direction VIEDEŇ -> BRATISLAVA from road E58 (border) direction Brno to Lafranconi bridge, turn to Mlynská dolina.
  • For your convenience, please use a GPS and navigate to: Bratislava, Ilkovičova 2, (Faculty of Informatics and Information Technologies), coordinates: 48.153429, 17.071716.

If you travel by car, there will be plenty of parking space available in front of the conference building.

Public transport

To get to the conference venue from bus station, take bus number 70 towards “Most SNP” and get off at stop “Most SNP”. The ride takes about 5 minutes, depending on the traffic. You have several options to change

  • bus number 30 or 37 and get off at stop “ZOO”. The ride takes 5-7 minutes, depending on the traffic.

or

  • bus number 28 or 29 or tram number 4, 5, 6 or 9 and get off at stop “Botanická záhrada”. The ride takes 6-9 minutes, depending on the traffic.

To get to the conference venue from train station, take bus number 32 towards “Dlhé diely” and get off at stop “ZOO”. The ride takes 12-15 minutes, depending on the traffic.

To get to the conference venue from the hotel Ibis, you need to go to the stop “Zochova” and take bus number 31 or 39 towards “Cintorín Slávičie údolie” and get off at the stop “ZOO”. The ride takes 8-10 minutes, depending on the traffic.

There will be signs from the bus stop towards the venue but please check Google Street View beforehand to be sure where you're going.

Public transport tickets

You can buy a ticket in yellow vending machines for tickets accepting euro coins, or in newsstands. There is a wide variety of tickets, you can buy a 90-cent ticket that is valid for 30 minutes or there is 24 to 72 hour tickets. You can change buses and trams freely with a valid ticket. You need to mark the ticket in a machine once you enter a bus or tram. Once the ticket is marked, it is valid for 30 minutes or whatever time you've purchased. You can change to another bus or tram (but do NOT mark the ticket again, only do it during the first ride). Students with ISIC cards can buy discounted tickets, more information on Bratislava Public Transport website.

SMS ticket

If you are using a phone with a Slovak carrier (SIM cards from other countries do not support this) you can buy tickets by sending empty SMS to number 1100 and you will receive SMS with the ticket in about 2 minutes. Price for the SMS ticket is EUR 1 and the ticket is valid for 70 minutes. Important: DO NOT enter the bus or tram after you send the SMS but ONLY after you receive the confirmation SMS. More information on Bratislava Public Transport website.

Other transport options

There are several Uber-like services operating in Bratislava: Uber, Hopin...

You can also take a regular taxi, however be aware that you should order a taxi from phone to your location. If you just “step in” a taxi on the street, the ride will be several times more expensive.

Accomodation

There is plenty of opportunities for accommodation in Bratislava. You can try from coach surfing, Airbnb through hostels to five star hotels. Even thou it is not the main tourist season, we advice you to book your accommodation in advance. Closest hotel to conference is hotel Družba. Most of the speakers will stay in hotel Ibis in town center. In this hotel, there will also be the Saturday night diner.

Guide

Bratislava offers much more than just PyCon, you can experience a lot of new things. If you decide to stay in Bratislava a few days longer, have a look at M_P_BA (alternative map of Bratislava) which lists popular as well as unusual destinations in the city along with people’s comments.

Or you can check the official city guide for visitors, where you can also find other events that are happening in Bratislava.
